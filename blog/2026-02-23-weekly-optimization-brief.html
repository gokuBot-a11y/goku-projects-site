3) Three optimization actions for next week

  1. Clamp routine-task verbosity: trim repeated planning/analysis loops on low-risk tasks and set a shorter default response profile for routine transforms.
    Why: current cost profile is input-dominant, so shorter prompts/loops have immediate impact.
  2. Stronger escalation gate to premium reasoning: keep low-cost/default lanes for routine execution; escalate only when task risk/ambiguity crosses a clear threshold.
    Why: aligns with standing research guidance to preserve quality while minimizing premium-token exposure.
  3. Expand local offload for bulk transforms: route repetitive summarization, cleanup, and formatting tasks to local fallback models first.
    Why: recent research shows local offload is the safest recurring lever for reducing paid-token pressure.
